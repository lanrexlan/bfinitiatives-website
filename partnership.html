Executive Summary

Dodja Errands Ltd. respectfully submits this proposal to explore a strategic partnership with your prestigious bank that advances both our platform's growth objectives and your institution's core mandate of economic development, youth employment creation, and SME support across Nigeria.

Rather than a conventional commercial arrangement, we propose a multi-dimensional collaboration that positions Dodja Errands as infrastructure supporting your beneficiary ecosystem. Our technology-enabled logistics platform can serve as both an income generation vehicle for individuals in your empowerment programs and a logistics solution for businesses within your lending portfolio.

This proposal outlines three strategic collaboration pathways, each designed to create measurable impact aligned with developmental banking objectives while establishing sustainable revenue streams for Dodja Errands. We propose initiating this partnership through a structured pilot program that demonstrates quantifiable outcomes before scaling across your broader beneficiary base.

About Dodja Errands

Dodja Errands operates as a technology-enabled connection platform that links users requiring pickup and delivery services with independent riders in their local areas. Unlike traditional logistics companies that maintain costly fleet infrastructure, our low-overhead marketplace model leverages proprietary matching algorithms to create efficient, transparent, and community-powered logistics solutions.

Dual Market Focus

Serving busy professionals and businesses seeking reliability, while providing flexible income opportunities for individuals.

Revenue Model

Riders retain 80-85% of fees. Dodja retains 15-20% for platform maintenance and technology infrastructure.

Core Values

Since our founding, we have been guided by core values of trust through technology, community-powered logistics, flexible opportunities, and transparent marketplace operations. These values align naturally with the developmental banking sector's commitment to inclusive economic growth.

Strategic Rationale

Developmental banks face consistent challenges in achieving their mandates: creating sustainable employment, ensuring SME viability, and extending financial services. Dodja Errands addresses each of these challenges through our existing platform capabilities:

1. Income Opportunities

Our rider network creates legitimate, flexible income opportunities that can be accessed by individuals with minimal capital requirements beyond a reliable means of transportation.

2. SME Efficiency

Our B2B logistics services can materially reduce operational costs for small businesses, improving their profitability and loan repayment capacity.

3. Community Presence

Our embedded presence in local communities positions us to serve as an extension point for banking services that require physical presence.

4. Value Proposition

Leveraging your institution's credibility and beneficiary networks while providing Dodja Errands with access to vetted individuals and businesses.

Proposed Collaboration Framework

We propose a three-pillar partnership structure that can be implemented sequentially or simultaneously based on your institutional priorities.

Pillar One: Youth & Beneficiary Empowerment

Concept: Positions Dodja Errands as an income generation solution for individuals already participating in your empowerment, entrepreneurship, or educational support programs.

  • Target: Youth in entrepreneurship programs, students, and vocational trainees.
  • Value to Bank: Creates documented employment outcomes and strengthens impact reporting.
  • Pilot Scale: 100 to 200 beneficiaries.
Pillar Two: SME Portfolio Logistics

Concept: Provides preferential logistics services to businesses within your lending portfolio, improving their operational efficiency.

  • Target: Retail, food service, small manufacturers, and agriculture.
  • Value to Bank: Improved operational efficiency reduces default risk and improves loan performance.
  • Pilot Scale: 20 to 30 businesses.
Pillar Three: Community Banking Extension

Concept: Leverages our rider network to extend basic banking services into communities where traditional branch infrastructure is uneconomical.

  • Activity: Account opening form collection, document collection, financial literacy distribution.
  • Pilot Scale: Single community with 10-15 specially trained riders.

Proposed Pilot Program Structure

We recommend initiating this partnership through a structured six-month pilot program focusing initially on Pillar One, with potential expansion into Pillar Two.

Phase One: Foundation (Month 1-2)

Jointly develop operational protocols, communication materials, and success metrics. Finalize beneficiary selection criteria and establish expedited onboarding procedures.

Phase Two: Launch and Activation (Month 3-4)

Focus on active beneficiary recruitment and platform activation. Conduct joint orientation sessions, process expedited rider onboarding, and initiate marketing to generate errand demand.

Phase Three: Optimization and Evaluation (Month 5-6)

Emphasize performance optimization. Collect usage and income data, conduct satisfaction surveys, and prepare a comprehensive pilot evaluation report.

Risk Mitigation & Expected Outcomes

Risk Mitigation

  • Reputational: All riders undergo mandatory background verification. Independent contractor model maintains liability separation.
  • Regulatory: Clear distinction between bank lending and logistics. No deposit-taking outside structured agency banking.
  • Financial: Minimal commitment; referral fees are success-based.

Expected Outcomes

  • Employment: A cohort of 150 riders could generate ₦2.4M - ₦4.5M aggregate income (Avg ₦25k-50k per rider).
  • Business Performance: Portfolio businesses typically see 20-35% logistics cost reductions.
  • Institutional Impact: Positions your institution as an innovative leader in developmental banking.
