Digital GreenTalents Award 2025

Dr. Adesola Habeeb Adegoke was selected as a Digital GreenTalent 2025 by the German Federal Ministry, recognising his contributions to sustainable development and digitalisation.

This award represents not just personal achievement, but an opportunity to bridge Nigerian talent with global sustainability initiatives.

Adesola Habeeb Adegoke
Background

His research explores how geotechnical engineering can be applied to solve environmental and social challenges, especially through work connected to the Salifu Lab.

The challenge

The GreenTalents initiative is highly competitive and looks for scientists whose work shows both technical strength and meaningful global potential.

Why it stood out

His integration of engineering with digital tools such as machine learning and sensors aligned directly with the programme's focus on sustainability through digitalisation.

What comes next

The award includes a three-month fully funded research stay in Germany in 2026 and participation in the Digital GreenTalents Summer School near Frankfurt.

Recognition includes

  • Selection as Digital GreenTalent 2025
  • Fully funded research residency in Germany
  • Access to the summer school cohort
  • Membership in a global sustainability network

Timeline

  • January 2025: Selected as a Digital GreenTalent
  • 2026: Research residency in Germany
  • Summer 2026: Specialised summer school near Frankfurt

Trent R. Dames & William W. Moore Fellowship

Named one of only two recipients nationwide of the prestigious 2025 fellowship from the American Society of Civil Engineers, recognising impactful geotechnical research.

This fellowship validates our efforts to push the boundaries of geotechnical engineering to solve real-world problems.

Adesola Habeeb Adegoke
Background

The fellowship supports graduate students using geotechnical engineering or earth science to address significant societal challenges.

The challenge

Competition spans top engineering programmes across the United States, with only two fellowships available nationwide.

Why it mattered

His work on sustainable infrastructure and resilience engineering aligned with the fellowship's mission and demonstrated strong long-term value.

Outcome

The recognition strengthens funding and visibility for the research being conducted in his lab and mentorship ecosystem.

Recognition includes

  • 2025 fellowship recipient
  • One of only two awardees nationwide
  • National validation of geotechnical research
  • Enhanced support for ongoing projects

Timeline

  • Late 2024: Submitted proposal and credentials
  • January 2025: Award notification
  • 2025: Fellowship implementation period
