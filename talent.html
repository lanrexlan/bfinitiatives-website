BFI Talent of the Year

Celebrating Nigerian excellence in scholarship, leadership, and impact.

The BFI Talent of the Year award honours exceptional Nigerians whose academic distinction, initiative, and contribution deserve wider recognition and meaningful support.

What it celebrates

Academic excellence with leadership and impact.

The award highlights talented Nigerians whose performance, initiative, and contribution signal uncommon promise.

About The Award

Recognition with practical momentum.

The BFI Talent of the Year award exists to identify and amplify high-performing Nigerians whose trajectory deserves both recognition and reinforcement.

Recognition

Visible celebration

The award spotlights scholars whose work and discipline deserve wider recognition.

Financial Award

Tangible support

Recognition is paired with support that can sustain educational growth and opportunity.

Networking

Meaningful access

Winners gain connection to a wider ecosystem of mentors, peers, and opportunity-builders.

Selection Criteria

What the award looks for.

01

Academic excellence

Strong demonstrated performance and commitment to rigorous scholarship.

02

Leadership & innovation

Evidence of initiative, problem-solving, and responsibility beyond the classroom.

03

Community impact

Work that improves other people's lives, opportunities, or environments.

04

Professional promise

The maturity and direction to translate talent into durable influence.

Past Winners

Honouring recent winners whose excellence continues to set the standard.

2023 Winner

Adewale Akinwale

Electrical Engineering

Seven WASSCE distinctions, including A1 grades in General Mathematics, Further Mathematics, and Biology.

Adewale distinguished himself through uncommon discipline, academic clarity, and a strong record of excellence. His recognition as the 2023 BFI Talent of the Year reflects both achievement and exceptional future promise.

2022 Winner

Tijani AbdulHafiz

BSc Chemical Engineering

A first-class Chemical Engineering graduate whose work brings together scholarship, mentorship, diversity in STEM, and sustainable energy research.

Tijani's profile stands out for pairing academic excellence with leadership and service. His 2022 recognition reflects a record of achievement already translating into meaningful societal impact.

2021 Winner

Alabere Hafsat

MRes Parasite Control, PhD Biomedical Science (in view)

A biomedical researcher advancing work in parasite control, malaria diagnosis, and SARS-CoV-2 inhibitor discovery.

Hafsat combines a first-class Microbiology foundation with internationally competitive research training and doctoral work. Her 2021 award recognised a trajectory defined by scientific rigour and global health relevance.

How Nomination Works

Simple on the surface, thoughtful underneath.

01

02

BFI reviews the evidence against the award criteria and overall fit.

03

Selection

Finalists and winners are chosen based on excellence, promise, and impact.
