BFI Talent of the Year Award

A national recognition for exceptional achievement in academic excellence and professional development among Nigerian students.

About the Award

Recognizing Exceptional Talent

The BFI Talent of the Year Award is our premier recognition program celebrating outstanding Nigerian students who demonstrate exceptional academic performance, leadership abilities, and commitment to community service.

This annual award identifies individuals who not only excel in their studies but also demonstrate the potential to become future leaders and change-makers in their fields and communities.

Recognition
National recognition through educational and corporate partners.
Financial Grant
Supporting continued educational and professional development.
Network Access
BFI's network of professionals, mentors, and alumni.

Excellence Standards

Selection Criteria

Evaluated across four dimensions to identify truly exceptional talent.

Academic Excellence

Minimum GPA of 4.5 or equivalent
Strong academic recommendations from faculty
Research publications or academic contributions

Leadership & Innovation

Demonstrated leadership in academic or extracurricular activities
Innovative project experience or creative solutions
Problem-solving with tangible outcomes

Community Impact

Volunteer work with measurable community benefits
Community service initiatives or programs
Social impact projects addressing local challenges

Professional Experience

Internship performance and recommendations
Work experience quality and field relevance
Professional achievements and recognitions

How to Nominate

Nomination Process

Transparent and merit-based. Three steps to recognize a deserving candidate.

1

Submission

Complete the online application with nominee's academic credentials, portfolio, and personal statement.

2

Evaluation

Applications reviewed by our selection committee against the four key criteria areas.

3

Selection

Finalists are interviewed, and the winner is selected based on overall excellence and impact.
